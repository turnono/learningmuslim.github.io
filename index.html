OUR INSTITUTE

Vision

Our aim is to enable English speakers to access the vast legacy of Islam by equipping them with the means to so. We are passionate about reviving the culture of learning and making Islamic knowledge accessible to all demographics within our community.

Syllabus

Each lesson comprises of 4 components (tasked to different teachers), Nahw (Grammar), Sarf (Morphology), Quraan Translation and an Arabic reader. Our unique syllabus is designed to maximise the understanding of core concepts in the shortest time possible. This is achieved by harmonizing theory and practical application to the study of the Quraan and Arabic readers while integrating learning assistive technologies such as Google Classroom and our own "Iraab" tool.

+
The primary aim of the course is to enable students who have no Arabic background to understand the Qur’an directly in the Arabic language. In order to achieve this, the course will equip the student with a foundation in Arabic grammar and morphology which will be used to undertake a word-by-word study of the Qur’an, its major concepts as well as its grammatical analysis. The course is based on a tried and tested methodology which has seen students gain the ability to understand the Quran directly in the Arabic language. Our students have been from diverse backgrounds and ages – ranging from high school children to grandparents.

* This is not a Quran recitation class

Presentation of Classes
The course is presented via MSPowerPoint.
Each lesson will be reinforced with numerous examples and exercises to ensure the student gains mastery of the concepts before moving on to the next one. This will also include homework tutorials.
Lessons are interactive, fun and spiritually uplifting with both group and individual learning.
Students are never singled out for questions and assessments are optional.
The learning environment is comfortable and relaxed.

Technology to aid learning – a “blended-learning approach”
Classes are integrated with Google Classroom and presentations are accessible to students if required.
All class audio along with presentation and study materials is uploaded every week to enable students to catch-up on missed lessons or review class material.
Concept videos are sent to students to prepare before class or review work covered – this frees up more time for interaction in class.
