OUR INSTITUTE

Vision

Our aim is to enable English speakers to access the vast legacy of Islam by equipping them with the means to do so. We are passionate about reviving the culture of learning and making Islamic knowledge accessible to all demographics within our community.

Syllabus

Each lesson comprises of 4 components (tasked to different teachers), Nahw (Grammar), Sarf (Morphology), Qur'aan Translation and an Arabic reader. Our syllabus is designed to maximise the understanding of core concepts in the shortest time possible. This is achieved by harmonizing theory and practical application to the study of the Qur'aan and Arabic readers while integrating learning assistive technologies such as Google Classroom™ and our own practical homework and assessment tool.

FAQ (Click on the question to expand each section below)

+
The primary aim of the course is to enable students who have no Arabic background to understand the Qur’aan directly in the Arabic language. In order to achieve this, the course will equip the student with a foundation in Arabic grammar and morphology which will be used to undertake a word-by-word study of the Qur’aan, its major concepts as well as its grammatical analysis. The course is based on a tried and tested methodology which has seen students gain the ability to understand the Qur'aan directly in the Arabic language. Our students have been from diverse backgrounds and ages – ranging from high school children to grandparents.

* This is not a Qur'aan recitation class

  • the desire to understand the Qur'aan directly in Arabic but have no Arabic background?
  • the basic ability to read the Qur'aan?
  • the commitment to attend lessons on Saturday's from 09:00 am to 12:50 pm?
  • the perseverance to dedicate 2 hours of revision time per week at home?
  • would like a learning environment that is comfortable, fun and uplifting?

This is your opportunity to join students from teens to grandparents who have undertaken this journey.
The course is open to both males and females!
Note that no Arabic background is required just a basic ability to read the Qur'aan.

No prior knowledge of Arabic is required. The course is aimed at complete beginners.
However, students are required to have a basic ability to read the Qur'aan although students will never be asked to read in class.
Students also need to have the commitment to revise the work covered every week as well as complete the homework exercises. This will require about 2 hours of revision every week.

Presentation of Classes
The course is presented via MS PowerPoint.
Each lesson will be reinforced with numerous examples and exercises to ensure that the student gains mastery of the concepts before moving on to the next one. This will also include homework tutorials.
Lessons are interactive, fun and spiritually uplifting with both group and individual learning.
Students are never singled out for questions and assessments are optional.
The learning environment is comfortable and relaxed.

Technology to aid learning – a “blended-learning approach”
Classes are integrated with Google Classroom™ and presentations are accessible to students if required.
All class audio along with presentation and study materials is uploaded every week to enable students to catch-up on missed lessons or review class material.
Concept videos are sent to students to prepare before class or review work covered – this frees up more time for interaction in class.

Academic Support
Questions can be sent via email and Whatsapp between classes.
